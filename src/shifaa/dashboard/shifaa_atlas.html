The Balance (Mizan)

Finding

Disease burden has a negative effect on trial activity (beta = -0.255, p < 0.001). Countries with more disease get fewer trials. GDP per capita is the strongest positive predictor (beta = +0.469, p = 0.004). Money, not need, determines where healing knowledge is generated.

Worst Projected Deterioration

DR Congo (51.2M DALYs), Myanmar (24.1M), Angola (16.2M), Madagascar (14.1M), and Chad (13.2M) face the steepest trajectories. Without targeted intervention, the next decade will deepen the divide between where disease kills and where evidence is generated.

The Binding Constraint

GDP per capita remains the binding constraint for the poorest nations. Even optimal governance is insufficient without direct research investment. The most responsive countries are those with existing but underfunded health infrastructure: Angola, Mozambique, and DR Congo.

The Reckoning (Hisab)

Finding

The Gini coefficient of trial distribution was 0.915 in 2005 and 0.893 in 2023 — extreme inequality comparable to the world's most unequal wealth distributions, showing negligible improvement over nearly two decades. COVID-19 produced no equity dividend.
