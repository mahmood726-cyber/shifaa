# Paper 4: The Path (Siraat)

## E156 Body (152 words, 7 sentences)

What would it take to close the evidence gap in the world's worst research deserts? Using the Poisson regression coefficients from our cross-country model, we computed counterfactual trial densities for the 30 largest evidence deserts under scenarios of improved governance and health expenditure. If governance quality in these countries reached the median of lower-middle-income nations and health expenditure rose to 5% of GDP, the model predicts a 3.2-fold increase in trial activity, potentially bringing 22 of the 30 countries above the zero-trial threshold. The most responsive countries to governance improvement were those with existing but underfunded health infrastructure, including Angola, Mozambique, and the Democratic Republic of Congo. GDP per capita remained the binding constraint for the poorest nations, where even optimal governance would be insufficient without direct research investment. These prescriptions identify modifiable levers for policy action rather than intractable structural barriers. Counterfactual estimates depend on the assumption that cross-sectional associations reflect achievable transitions.
