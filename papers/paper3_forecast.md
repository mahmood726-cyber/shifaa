# Paper 3: The Forecast (Qadr)

## E156 Body (150 words, 7 sentences)

Will the global research equity gap narrow or widen over the next decade? We projected the Research Equity Index from 2019 to 2036 for 180 countries using IHME burden growth trajectories and observed trial activity trends from ClinicalTrials.gov, incorporating the governance threshold effect identified by generalized additive modelling. Under baseline assumptions of 2% annual DALY growth in low-income countries and 1% trial growth, the 37 countries with over one million DALYs and zero trials in 2019 face a projected 30% widening of the mercy gap by 2036. The governance threshold at 0.38 means that 127 countries currently below this tipping point face a structural barrier: even modest burden growth cannot be matched by trial activity until institutional capacity crosses this threshold. Sub-Saharan Africa bears the steepest trajectory, with the Democratic Republic of Congo, Chad, Madagascar, Mozambique, and Niger facing the worst projected deterioration. Without targeted intervention to redirect research investment, the next decade will deepen the divide between where disease kills and where evidence is generated. Projections assume stable geopolitical conditions and linear extrapolation of current trends.
