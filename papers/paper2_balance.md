# Paper 2: The Balance (Mizan)

## E156 Body (154 words, 7 sentences)

What determines where clinical trials are conducted — disease burden or national wealth? We fitted a Poisson regression with generalised estimating equations to trial counts across 158 countries in 2019, adjusting for population, disease burden, GDP per capita, governance quality, health expenditure, human capital index, and physician density. Disease burden had a significant negative coefficient (beta=-0.255, p<0.001), meaning countries with higher DALYs had fewer trials per capita after adjusting for economic factors. GDP per capita was the strongest positive predictor (beta=+0.469, p=0.004), followed by health expenditure as percentage of GDP (beta=+0.081, p<0.001). Governance quality showed a positive but non-significant association (beta=+0.233, p=0.27), suggesting that institutional capacity matters less than raw economic resources. The model demonstrates that the global clinical trial enterprise systematically directs research investment away from populations with the greatest health needs. These findings are cross-sectional and cannot establish temporal causation between economic development and research activity.
