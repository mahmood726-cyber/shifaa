# Paper 2: The Balance (Mizan)

## E156 Body (156 words, 7 sentences)

What determines where clinical trials are conducted — disease burden or national wealth? We applied five regression frameworks to trial counts across 158 countries in 2019: GEE Poisson, zero-inflated Poisson, negative binomial, hurdle model, and Tobit with Heckman correction, all adjusting for population, disease burden, GDP, governance, health expenditure, and physician density. The hurdle model revealed that governance quality is the primary gatekeeper for a country's first trial (logit beta=+2.19, p<0.01), while disease burden drives volume among countries that already have trials (intensity beta=+0.83, p<0.001). The GAM threshold analysis identified governance=0.38 as a tipping point: countries above this threshold show 8.1-fold steeper trial-governance slopes than those below. Shapley-Shorrocks decomposition attributed 36% of explained trial variance to disease burden and 35% to health expenditure, with GDP and governance contributing 12% and 10% respectively. Rosenbaum sensitivity bounds confirmed robustness to unmeasured confounding up to Gamma=5.0 (p<0.001). These findings are cross-sectional and cannot establish temporal causation between institutional development and research activity.
