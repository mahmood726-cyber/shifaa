# Paper 1: The Mercy Map (Rahma)

## E156 Body (155 words, 7 sentences)

Where does clinical research reach the populations that need it most, and where does it fail them? We linked 74,881 site-weighted clinical trials from ClinicalTrials.gov to all-cause disability-adjusted life years from the IHME Global Burden of Disease study across 180 countries in 2019, computing a Research Equity Index as the log-ratio of trial density to disease burden per 100,000 population. Of 180 countries analysed, 72 had zero registered clinical trials despite collectively bearing 11.2% of global DALYs, with the Democratic Republic of Congo (51.2 million DALYs), Myanmar (24.1 million), and Angola (16.2 million) representing the largest evidence deserts. Moran's I confirmed significant spatial clustering of evidence deserts (I=0.25, p<0.0001), concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. The Esteban-Ray polarization index revealed two distinct regimes: 67% of countries averaging 0.5 trials versus 33% averaging 65, indicating structural bifurcation rather than gradual inequality. These findings quantify a global mercy gap in which healing knowledge flows toward wealth rather than toward suffering. This analysis is limited to ClinicalTrials.gov and may undercount trials registered exclusively in regional registries.
