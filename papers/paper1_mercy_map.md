# Paper 1: The Mercy Map (Rahma)

## E156 Body (156 words, 7 sentences)

Where does clinical research reach the populations that need it most, and where does it fail them? We linked 74,881 site-weighted clinical trials from ClinicalTrials.gov to all-cause disability-adjusted life years from the IHME Global Burden of Disease study across 180 countries in 2019, computing a Research Equity Index as the log-ratio of trial density to disease burden per 100,000 population. Of 180 countries analysed, 72 had zero registered clinical trials despite collectively bearing 28% of global DALYs, with the Democratic Republic of Congo (51.2 million DALYs), Myanmar (24.1 million), and Angola (16.2 million) representing the largest evidence deserts. The United States alone concentrated 1,281 weighted trial-equivalents, more than the bottom 120 countries combined. Trial density correlated with national income (Spearman rho=0.74, p<0.001) rather than disease burden (rho=-0.18, p=0.02). These findings quantify a global mercy gap in which healing knowledge flows toward wealth rather than toward suffering. This analysis is limited to ClinicalTrials.gov and may undercount trials registered exclusively in regional registries.
