## E156 Body (Paper 1 — The Mercy Map)

Where does healing knowledge reach the people who need it most, and where does it fail them? We linked 578,000 ClinicalTrials.gov studies to IHME Global Burden of Disease DALYs for the 50 causes responsible for 85% of global disability, mapping trial activity to disease burden across 190 countries from 2005 to 2025. A site-weighted Research Equity Index quantifies the ratio of clinical research density to disease burden per 100,000 population for every country-disease pair, adjusted for World Bank governance quality, health expenditure, and WHO health system capacity. The 50 worst evidence deserts — country-disease pairs with the lowest REI — concentrate in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia for conditions including ischemic heart disease, lower respiratory infections, and neonatal disorders. Multilevel Poisson regression reveals that governance quality explains more variance in trial distribution than disease burden itself, suggesting that political and institutional factors, not epidemiological need, determine where healing knowledge is generated. The primary limitation is reliance on ClinicalTrials.gov as a proxy for global research activity, which underrepresents trials registered in regional registries.
